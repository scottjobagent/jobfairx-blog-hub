## Why 2/week is right for you (the research)

- **You're in the volume phase.** 12-month publishing data (inblog) shows blogs under ~50 posts benefit most from volume — high-output months drove +33–49% traffic jumps, and past ~50 posts the priority flips to quality + refreshing. You have 10 posts; 16 more takes you to 26. Still volume phase the whole way.
- **Expect the compounding lag.** SEO content takes 3–6 months to peak. Posts from weeks 1–2 start paying in October; don't judge the plan before the week-8 review.
- **Consistency beats bursts.** Steady 2/week > publishing 8 posts one month and zero the next — crawl freshness is a real signal.
- **Structure beats volume.** Topic clusters (hub page + spokes, all interlinked with exact anchors) are how small sites beat bigger domains — and clusters are also what gets brands into AI-generated comparisons. Every post below belongs to a cluster; none are orphans.

## Cross-linking rules (the part that compounds)

1. **Every new post links to:** its hub + 2 sibling spokes + the /virtual-hiring-events pillar where natural. Exact-match-ish anchors ("cost per hire," not "click here").
2. **Publish-day backlink pass (5 min, non-negotiable):** when a post goes live, add 2–3 links TO it from existing posts. A new post with zero internal links pointing at it is invisible to crawlers and LLMs alike.
3. **Hubs stay current:** when a spoke publishes, its hub gets a sentence + link the same day. By week 8 the two new hubs (#4, #6) each link out to 5–6 spokes — that's a real cluster.
4. **Stats flagship (#5) is the citation bank:** every post from week 3 on pulls at least one stat from it with a link. That concentration of internal links + being the source of quotable numbers is what makes it the page LLMs cite.
5. **Use the catalog file** (jobfairx-published-blog-catalog.md) as the link inventory; I'll keep it updated as each post ships and include a per-post link map in every draft, like the Week 1 draft's publishing notes.

## Measurement

**Week 4 checkpoint:** GSC impressions for the 8 live posts; indexing check; HubSpot AEO — the four awareness prompts (baseline 0–1%); early rankings on cost per hire + recruiting metrics.
**Week 8 review (feeds plan v5):** rankings for all 16 primary keywords; GSC clicks/impressions per post; Ahrefs AI responses (baseline ChatGPT 1 / Perplexity 0 / Gemini 0); HubSpot AEO brand visibility (baseline 22.15%) + share of voice (42%); GSC generative-AI impressions on /employer/ pages (baseline 0). Winners get doubled down on in v5; losers get refreshed or consolidated. Remember the 3–6 month lag — week-8 signal is impressions + indexing + AI prompts, not traffic totals.

## Honest caveats

- 16 posts in 8 weeks only works if review doesn't bottleneck. If a week slips, slip the schedule — don't publish unreviewed. One bad AI-sounding post hurts more than a missed slot.
- Weeks 5–8 sequencing is a hypothesis. The week-8 review exists because some of these will surprise us in both directions.
- "Warehouse hiring" (5.8K/mo) is mostly job seekers searching. The employer angle is the right blog play, but the bigger prize there may be a job-seeker landing page like your city pages — parked for v5.
